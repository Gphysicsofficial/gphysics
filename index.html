About the Competition

GPhysics is a one-day, online physics competition created specifically for middle school girls (grades 6–8). It’s built to be fun, challenging, and totally beginner-friendly. Whether you’ve taken a physics class or not, this is a great way to dive into science in a hands-on, creative way.

How It Works

  • Individual Round: Solve 15 multiple-choice and short-answer questions on your own. These questions range from basic motion and forces to energy and waves — no calculators or complicated math needed.
  • Team Round: Work with up to 3 friends (or be matched with others) to tackle open-ended problems. You’ll brainstorm ideas, write out solutions, and come up with cool experiments or designs — all virtually.
  • Relay Round: A fast-paced challenge where your team solves a chain of problems one after another. Communication and teamwork are key!

What You’ll Need

  • A laptop, tablet, or computer with internet access
  • A curious mind and willingness to try something new
  • Optional: paper and pencil, and a basic calculator

What Makes GPhysics Special?

  • Created by students, for students — we get what makes competitions feel stressful or fun
  • All problems are designed with middle schoolers in mind — no physics class required
  • There are workshops beforehand to help you feel prepared
  • We care more about creativity, teamwork, and curiosity than perfect scores

GPhysics is a place to experiment, explore, and grow. If you’ve ever wondered what physics is like — or never thought it was for you — this is your chance to jump in.

FAQ

Who can participate?

Any girl in grades 6–8, anywhere in the world! You just need internet access and a curiosity about how things work.

Do I need to be good at physics to join?

Not at all! GPhysics is for everyone — from total beginners to science lovers. If you're curious and willing to try, you belong here.

Does it cost anything?

Nope! GPhysics is 100% free to join.

What if I don’t have a team?

No problem! You can sign up solo. We'll help match solo participants into teams if they want to join one for the Team Round.

How do I prepare?

You can attend our free workshops, check out past problems, and just explore the world around you. Physics is everywhere!
